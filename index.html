Kensington Opportunity
Kensington Opportunity

This map identifies a potential site for a new healthy food grocery store in Philadelphia’s American Street Empowerment Zone. The analysis focuses on areas that are accessible by public transit while avoiding overlap with existing farmers markets and Healthy Corner Store Initiative locations. The selected area offers a large contiguous site, making it more realistic for future development. This project demonstrates how GIS can be used to connect food access, public investment, and site selection.

Map 2
Risk of Poison
Risk of Poison

This map studies drinking water risk in Flint, Michigan by locating areas where environmental hazards and aging infrastructure overlap. High-risk zones were created by comparing polluted industrial facilities, river corridors, and lead service line locations. The map reveals how toxic exposure is not evenly distributed across the city. Through GIS analysis, the project shows how spatial data can help identify communities that may need urgent infrastructure repair and public health intervention.

Map 3
Covid Recoveries
Covid Recoveries

This map visualizes patterns of COVID-19 recovery across different locations. The project uses GIS to compare how health outcomes vary spatially rather than treating recovery as a single overall statistic. By mapping the data, the analysis makes visible the uneven impacts of the pandemic across communities. The project demonstrates how GIS can be used to communicate public health information clearly and support a better understanding of regional differences.
